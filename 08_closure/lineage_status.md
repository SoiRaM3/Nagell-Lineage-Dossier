## Documentary Status

The Nagell family is documented as a medieval armigerous lineage in Norway by
the mid-15th century, with continuity into the early modern period.

The lineage is traceable through:
- surname usage
- heraldic association
- institutional roles
- documentary references

---

## Surname Continuity

Based on currently available documentation, the **Nagell surname line appears to
terminate with the Stokknes family as the last known name-bearers**.

This conclusion is based solely on observable surname transmission in the
historical record.

---

## Clarifications

This status does **not** imply:
- extinction of biological descendants
- loss of historical significance
- transfer of authority or representation
- custodianship or succession of social role

It reflects documentation only.

---

## Lineage Beyond the Surname

While surname usage may terminate, lineage in a broader biological or cultural
sense may continue under different names.

Such continuation is common and does not alter the documentary status of the
surname line itself.

## Status Summary

The Nagell lineage is best described as:
- historically attested
- armigerous
- continuous through the early modern period
- surname-terminated in available records
- preserved through documentation rather than authority
