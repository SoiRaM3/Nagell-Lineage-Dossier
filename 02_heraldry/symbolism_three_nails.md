## Canting Arms

The use of **three nails** in the Nagell coat of arms is best understood as a
form of **canting heraldry**.

Canting arms visually reference a family name through:
- phonetic similarity  
- linguistic association  
- symbolic representation  

In this case, the nails reference the Germanic word *nagel*, aligning name and
symbol.

---

## Medieval Heraldic Practice

In medieval Europe, canting arms served practical purposes:

- visual identification in seals and banners  
- reinforcement of lineage identity  
- ease of recognition across regions  

Such designs were common and should not be interpreted as allegorical or
religious unless explicitly documented.

---

## Avoidance of Symbolic Overreach

While nails can carry religious or metaphorical meanings in other contexts,
there is **no documentary basis** for assigning such interpretations to the
Nagell arms.

Within this repository, the nails are treated strictly as:
- a heraldic charge  
- a linguistic reference  
- an identity marker  

No symbolic meaning beyond this is asserted.

---

## Comparative Context

Similar canting motifs appear across medieval heraldry, including:

- tools  
- animals  
- objects  
- letters or shapes  

The Nagell arms fit squarely within this broader European tradition.
