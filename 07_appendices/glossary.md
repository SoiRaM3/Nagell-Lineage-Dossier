# Glossary

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Nagell lineage dossier.
Definitions are descriptive and aligned with historical usage.

---

**Armigerous**  
A family or individual recognized as bearing a coat of arms. Armigerous status
does not necessarily imply noble rank.

---

**Canting Arms**

---

**Coat of Arms**  
A hereditary heraldic device used for identification in medieval and early
modern Europe.

---

**Early Modern Period**  
The period roughly spanning the late 15th to the 18th century, characterized by
state centralization, Reformation, and expanding bureaucracy.

---

**Heraldry**  
The system of symbols, rules, and practices governing coats of arms and related
identifiers.

---

**Lineage**  
A line of descent or identity recognized through historical, cultural, or
documentary continuity.

---

**Name-bearer**  
An individual documented as using a particular surname. The term does not imply
authority, representation, or inheritance beyond name usage.

---

**Reformation**  
The religious and institutional transformation of Christianity in Europe during
the 16th century, with significant social and political consequences.

---

**Surname Termination**  
The point at which a surname no longer appears in independent transmission
within available documentation.

---

**Uradel**  
A term used in some European historiography to describe noble lineages whose
status predates formal ennoblement systems. Use of the term is descriptive and
context-dependent.

---

## Note

Terms are used consistently throughout the repository to avoid ambiguity or
anachronism.
