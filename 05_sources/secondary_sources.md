# Secondary Sources

This document outlines **secondary sources** consulted or referenced indirectly
in compiling the Nagell lineage dossier.

Secondary sources are treated as interpretive aids rather than primary evidence.

---

## Types of Secondary Sources

Relevant secondary material includes:
- published genealogies
- regional historical studies
- heraldic surveys
- scholarly analyses of medieval Scandinavia
- early modern administrative histories

---

## Use and Limitations

Secondary sources are used to:
- contextualize primary evidence
- identify potential leads
- compare interpretations

They are not treated as authoritative unless corroborated.

---

## Methodological Note

Later genealogical compilations are approached cautiously, particularly where
they:
- lack citation
- conflict with primary documentation
- incorporate speculative links
